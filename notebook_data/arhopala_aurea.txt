Arhopala aurea belongs to the aurea group. It is a robust species characterized by its distinctive structural features and specific spot alignments on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Forewing Markings: The dark patch typically found under the cell in space 1b is not solid.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Structural Features:
The hindwing cell is exceptionally long, measuring considerably more than half the length of the wing in the male.

It possesses a short and stout tail at vein 2, measuring just under 2.0 mm.

Similar Species: It closely resembles Arhopala borneensis on the underside, but they are distinguished by their upperside features.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even inside, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlapping: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Green with a weak blue flush at the wing bases. The black border on the forewing measures 0.75–1.5 mm at the tornus and decreases to a thread above vein 3 or 4. On the hindwing, the green area does not reach the termen.

Female: Some shade of blue with a comparatively narrow black border on the hindwing, which reaches less than halfway to the end of the cell.
