Arhopala eumolphus is the namesake member of the eumolphus group. It is a moderately large, tailed species characterized by its hair brown underside and specific spot arrangements.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Postdiscal Band: The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted outward (distad), moving it out of line with the spots in spaces 3 and 5.

Hindwing Postdiscal Band: The band is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Ground Colour: The underside ground colour is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are broad and typically well-defined.

Forewing Shape: The forewing is rounded, distinguishing it from the more acute apex seen in Arhopala hellenore.

Size: The forewing length typically ranges from 21.0 mm to 23.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned directly below the spot in space 7.

Shape: The spot is usually outwardly concave, which serves as a diagnostic feature to separate it from related blue/purple species in the group whose spot 6 is often outwardly straight.

Arrangement: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line. The inner edge of the spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Rather brassy green with a black border that decreases from 2.0 mm at the tornus to 1.0 mm at the apex.

Female: Purple blue with broad borders that usually reach the apex of the forewing cell.
