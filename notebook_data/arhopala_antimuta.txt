Arhopala antimuta is a common species belonging to the amphimuta group and the perimuta subgroup. It is distinguished by its tailless hindwing and specific spot arrangements on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: 
The centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

The spot in space 6 is positioned directly below the spot in space 7.

Its inner edge is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is completely dislocated at vein 2, though this character can occasionally be inconstant.

Tornal Area: The hindwing features green tornal scales.

Tail Status: It is a tailless species.

Ground Color: The underside is uniform hair brown.

Size: A relatively small species with a forewing length of approximately 16.0 mm.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deep, shining violet blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Shining blue with a variable purple tinge and typical broad black borders.
