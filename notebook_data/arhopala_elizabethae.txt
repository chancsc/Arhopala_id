Arhopala elizabethae is a very rare and small species belonging to the ganesa group. It is remarkably similar to Arhopala ariel but can be distinguished by its upperside coloration and specific underside spot arrangements.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
Possesses a characteristic spot at the base of space 11, situated between the basal and central cell spots.

Features a spot at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
Tornal green scales are present.

The outer and central spots in space 7 are joined at their upper ends.

The gap between these spots in space 7 is, at most, only partially white (distinguishing it from A. ammonides, where the gap is completely filled with white).

Ground Colour: Typically a shade of purple brown.

Size: A very small species with a forewing length of approximately 13.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the ganesa group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Extent: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is described as "normal," meaning it does not reach the base of the space. This diagnostic feature separates it from Arhopala ammon, where spot 6 is continued all the way to the base of the space.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining, deep blue with black borders that are slightly wider than those of Arhopala ariel.

Female: Generally similar to the male.
