Arhopala abseus is the namesake member of the abseus group. It is distinguished by its unique tail arrangement and specific spot configurations on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys and Features

Hindwing Tails: This species is characterized by having a short, white-tipped tail at the end of vein 3, in addition to the usual tail at vein 2.

Marking Coloration: The markings on the underside are prominently darkened, often appearing blackish brown.

Hindwing Spot 1a: A characteristic feature of this group is the presence of a spot at the base of space 1a on the hindwing underside.

Hindwing Spot 7: The spot located in mid-space 7 is distinctly outlined with whitish scaling.

Forewing Costal Spots: It possesses a costal spot in space 12, as well as two spots in each of spaces 9, 10, and 11.

Forewing Cell Markings: Like the ganesa group, it features spots above the cell and a spot at the base of space 3.

Size: It is a relatively small species with a forewing length of approximately 17.0 mm.

Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6 Position

Alignment: The centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

End-cell Bar: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: The upperside is a lustrous violet blue with a black border that expands from 2.0 mm at the tornus to 4.0 mm at the apex.

Female: The female is a shining blue with even wider black borders than the male.
