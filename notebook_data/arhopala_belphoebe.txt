Arhopala belphoebe cowani is a rare member of the camdeo group. It is characterized by having the sexes alike on the upperside and lacking a hindwing tail.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Band: The postdiscal band is incomplete and ends at vein 7.

Forewing Markings: It features a dark area positioned under the cell that is outwardly defined by a pale line normally stretching right across space 1b. 

Subcostal Spots: It typically lacks a subcostal spot above the central cell spot in space 11 or at the base of space 10.

Structural Features: It is a tailless species.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of approximately 18.0–20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the camdeo group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Sexes: The male and female are alike in appearance.

Colour: Pale violet blue, which is much lighter than the related Arhopala johoreana.

Borders: Both sexes have broad black borders that expand on the forewing to about 5.0 mm at the costa.
