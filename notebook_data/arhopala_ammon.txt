Arhopala ammon ammon is a small, rare species belonging to the ganesa group. It is most frequently seen in Singapore but remains a rarity elsewhere in its range.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
Possesses a small spot at the base of space 11, positioned between the basal and central cell spots.

A spot is typically present at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
Contains tornal green scales, which distinguish it from Arhopala ammonides.

The gap between the central and outer spots in space 7 is whitened.

Structural Features: It has a short tail at vein 2.

Size: Small species, with a forewing length of approximately 16.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the ganesa group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Specific Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is continued to the extreme base of the space. This is a key diagnostic feature separating it from Arhopala ariel and Arhopala elizabethae, where the spot is "normal" and does not reach the base of the space.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark blue with narrow black borders, measuring approximately 1.5 mm at the forewing apex.

Female: Features narrow black borders, approximately 2.0 mm wide at the forewing tornus and on the hindwing.
