Arhopala aroa is a member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is characterized by its rounded wing shape and the specific spacing of its underside markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are not conspicuously darker than the ground colour and are outlined by pale buff striae.

Postdiscal Band Proximity: The postdiscal bands are usually narrow and are well separated (placed well clear) from the inner submarginal lunules on both wings.

Wing Structure: The wings are rather elongate, and the forewing has a rounded termen.

Forewing Length: 18.0–21.0 mm

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the alea subgroup, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Shape: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is notably much narrower at the bottom than at the top.

Placement relative to End-Cell Bar: This spot usually barely overlaps the end-cell bar.

Placement relative to Spot 5: The outer edge of spot 6 is typically positioned well inside the inner edge of the spot in space 5.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deeper violet than the related Arhopala aenigma.

Female: The black forewing border is narrow, decreases in width towards the tornus, and does not reach the cell apex.
