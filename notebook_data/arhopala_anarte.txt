Arhopala anarte is a member of the camdeo group and the camdeo subgroup. It is notable for being the Malayan Arhopala species with the greatest wing expanse.

Underside Identification Keys

Size: This is a very large species, significantly larger than most other members of the genus.

Forewing Markings:
It possesses a characteristic central spot in space 11.

There is a spot at the base of space 10.

The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 4, with the spots in spaces 4, 5, and 6 being in line and oblique.

General Pattern: The underside ground colour is typical for the group (hair brown), with darker spots outlined by pale striae.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the camdeo group, the centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon and form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: A beautiful, shining, pale silvery blue that is overlaid with violet towards the costa and termen of both wings; the black border is reduced to a thread.

Female: Violet blue with the broad black borders typical of the genus.
