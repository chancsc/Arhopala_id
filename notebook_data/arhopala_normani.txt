Arhopala normani is a rare member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is particularly difficult to identify and closely resembles Arhopala sublustris, though the male is a less shining and more violet blue.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is hair brown and lacks a slatey glaze.

Marking Definition: The markings are not conspicuously darker than the ground colour and are outlined by pale buff striae.

Band Placement: The postdiscal band is positioned very close to the inner submarginal lunules on both wings.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of 17.0–19.0 mm.

Wing Shape: The wing shape is consistent with related species like A. sublustris and A. phanda.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the alea subgroup, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Overlap: The postdiscal spot in space 6 characteristically widely overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: Unlike its relative A. aroa, the spot in space 6 is usually not much narrower at the bottom than at the top.

Placement relative to Spot 5: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned well inside the inner edge of the spot in space 5.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Blue with a violet tinge that is not very strong. The black forewing border is narrow and regular, not more than 0.5 mm wide.

Female: Possesses the typical broad black borders of the alea subgroup, which generally measure about 2.0 mm wide from the tornus to vein 4.
