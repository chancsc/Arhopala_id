Arhopala pseudomuta (Raffles's Oakblue) is a member of the alitaeus group. Its identification on the underside is based on specific spot alignments and subtle color characteristics.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are notably faint, being hardly darker than the ground colour.

Forewing Markings: 
It possesses a central spot in space 11.

A spot at the extreme base of space 10 (above the central cell spot) may be present or absent.

The postdiscal spot in space 1b is often dislocated outwards from the spot in space 2, whereas in most related species, these spots are continuous and oblique.

Hindwing Markings: The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Tail: It is a tailed species.

Subspecies Variation: 
Subspecies pseudomuta has a hair brown underside.

The Langkawi subspecies ariavana has a darker underside that may be purple-washed.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even inside, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: Unlike many other species in the genus, the postdiscal spot in space 6 is usually slightly oblique and does not overlap the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male (pseudomuta): Violet blue with a black border approximately 1.0 mm wide.

Male (ariavana): Features a broader black border.

Female: Displays typical broad black borders.
