Arhopala sublustris ridleyi is a member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is known for being part of a subgroup that presents significant difficulties in identification due to high similarity among species.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are not conspicuously darker than the ground color and are outlined by pale buff striae.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species with a filamentous tail at vein 2. It possesses a less conspicuous tornal lobe compared to members of the cleander subgroup.

Comparison: It most closely resembles Arhopala normani on the underside, but they can be distinguished by the upperside color of the male (shining blue in A. sublustris vs. violet in A. normani).

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the alea subgroup, the centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Shape: Spot 6 is narrower at the bottom than at the top, with an outer edge that is convex or sinuous.

Placement: The outer edge of spot 6 is placed well inside the inner edge of spot 5. This is a diagnostic feature used to separate it from species where these spots might overlap or be more aligned.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: A rich, shining blue with a black border measuring slightly less than 0.5 mm.

Female: Blue with a black forewing border usually about 2.0 mm wide from vein 4 to the tornus.
