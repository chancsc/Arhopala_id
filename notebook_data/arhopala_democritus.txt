Arhopala democritus belongs to the democritus group and is characterized by specific spot alignments and a short tail.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color and Markings: The underside patterns vary by subspecies:
Subspecies lycaenaria: Ground color is hair brown with pale buff striae. This is the common form in the Malay Peninsula.

Subspecies democritus: Ground color is dark chocolate with markings outlined by white striae and dots, often making the pattern difficult to discern.

Forewing Markings:
Possesses a costal spot in mid-space 11.

The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted distad (outwards), moving it out of line with the spots in spaces 5 and 6.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Tail: It possesses a short tail, measuring approximately 1.5–2.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

End-cell Bar: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The spot is notably outwardly concave.

Upperside Characteristics

Male (lycaenaria): Purple blue with narrow borders.

Male (democritus): Much lighter and more silvery blue.
