Arhopala alaconia media belongs to the agelastus group and is the only member of the alesia subgroup. It is a rare species that can be distinguished from its relatives by the specific enlargement of its submarginal markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Enlarged Markings: The inner submarginal spots in spaces 2 and 3 on the forewing, as well as those in spaces 4 and 5 on the hindwing, are distinctly enlarged and darkened.

Forewing Markings:
The postdiscal band is continuous.

A costal spot in space 10 is typically absent.

A small spot is often present at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
The postdiscal band is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Tornal green scales are present.

Structural Features: It is typically a tailless species (briefly toothed at vein 2), though a very rare form (f. kempi) exists with a tail approximately 2.5 mm long.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the agelastus group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Shape: The outer edge of the spot in space 6 is concave or sinuous. This is a key diagnostic feature, as other members of the agelastus group possess an outwardly convex spot 6.

Placement: The inner edge of spot 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Sexes Alike: Unlike many Arhopala, the male and female are similar in appearance. Both are violet blue with very broad black borders that expand to about 3.0 mm at the forewing tornus and reach the mid-costa.
