Arhopala kurzi belongs to the amphimuta group and is the sole member of the kurzi subgroup. It is a moderately large, tailless species primarily found in lowland forests.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Structure: The hindwing tornus is rounded with a very small tornal lobe. The cilia are prolonged at vein 2 into a very short, often inconspicuous tooth.

Hindwing Cilia: The white spot in space 1a of the hindwing cilia is typically absent or, very rarely, obscurely present.

Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is particularly widely dislocated at vein 2.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal band is variable and its lower part is sometimes obsolete. When fully developed, the spot in space 1b is shifted inwards, positioned midway between the postdiscal spot in space 2 and the end-cell bar.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are overlapping and arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line).

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Rather dull purple-blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Purple with a black hindwing border that is unusually variable; the blue color sometimes reaches the termen between veins 2 and 5, while in other individuals it is almost entirely confined to the cell.
