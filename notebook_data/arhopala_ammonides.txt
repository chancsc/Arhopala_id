Arhopala ammonides is a small species (forewing length ~14.0 mm) belonging to the ganesa group. Its identification on the underside is characterized by specific markings and the absence of tornal scales.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is purple-brown.

Forewing Markings: It possesses a spot in space 11 between the basal and central cell spots, as well as a spot at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
Space 7: There is a distinct white area filling the gap between the central and outer spots in space 7.

Tornal Area: It lacks green tornal scales.

Postdiscal Band: The band is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Subspecies: The mainland form is A. ammonides chunsu, while the Langkawi form is A. ammonides monava.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even inside, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlapping: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.
