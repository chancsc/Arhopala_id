Flos morphina (also known as Arhopala morphina) is a distinctive member of the genus, characterized by its tailless hindwing and a very specific basal pattern.

Underside Identification Keys and Features

Basal Area: The species is instantly recognizable by a sharply defined, solid, brownish-black basal area on the hindwing underside.

Hindwing Shape: The hindwing is tailless and has an evenly rounded termen.

Missing Markings: 
It lacks a dark marginal spot in space 2 of the hindwing.

It lacks the brassy metallic scales at the hindwing tornus that are found in species like Flos apidanus.

Marking Coloration: The underside markings are dark purple-brown, though they do not contrast as strongly with the ground color as those of F. diardi or F. fulgida.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

End-cell Bar: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: A beautiful lustrous blue with a black border that is reduced to a thread.

Female: Shining purplish blue with relatively narrow black borders.
