Arhopala kinabala is a member of the agesias group. It is characterized by prominent rounded spots on the underside and a tailless, though slightly dentate, hindwing.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is elongate and overlaps the end-cell bar.

Marking Coloration: The underside features large, blackish-brown markings on a hair-brown ground.

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal spot in space 3 is elongate and touches the end-cell bar.

It possesses two spots in space 11 (distinguishing it from A. agesias, which has only one).

Hindwing Spot 7: The postdiscal spot in space 7 is also large and prominent.

Upperside Characteristics

Sexes Alike: Both male and female are a shining, rather powdery blue.

Borders: Both wings have broad black borders (about 1.5 mm at the forewing tornus) that expand to the mid-costa on the forewing.
