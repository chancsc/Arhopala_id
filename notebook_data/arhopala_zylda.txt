Arhopala zylda elioti is a rare and small member of the amphimuta group. It is distinguished from related species by its specific size and the alignment of its postdiscal markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Shape: The hindwing is evenly rounded.

Cilia and Wing Edge: The hindwing cilia lack any trace of a tooth at vein 2.

Postdiscal Band: On the hindwing, the postdiscal band is characteristically partially dislocated at vein 2. (Note: This helps separate it from species where the band is completely dislocated).

Marking Style: The markings consist of quadrate and banded spots on a hair brown ground colour.

Size: A small species with a forewing length of 16.0–17.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: Following the amphimuta group pattern, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining blue with a slight violet tinge and a regular black border measuring 1.0 mm.

Female: Paler blue than the male, with all black borders notably narrower than is usual for the genus.
