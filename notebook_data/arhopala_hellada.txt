Arhopala hellada ozana is a large species belonging to the camdeo group and the camdeo subgroup. It was historically confused with Arhopala aedias, but it can be distinguished by its wing shape, tail length, and specific underside spot arrangements.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
Features a dark area under the cell, outwardly defined by a pale line that normally stretches right across space 1b.

The postdiscal band is complete and only partially dislocated at vein 4.

The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted distad (outwards), placing it out of line with the spots in spaces 5 and 6.

It usually possesses a small, tell-tale spot at the extreme base of space 3.

A costal spot in space 10 is rarely present.

Hindwing Markings:
The postdiscal band is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Structural Features: It possesses a very short tail, which helps differentiate it from the longer-tailed A. aedias.

Size: A large species with a forewing length of approximately 28.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the camdeo group, the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, with their centers forming a more or less straight line.

Placement: The spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Clear violet blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: More purplish blue than the male.
