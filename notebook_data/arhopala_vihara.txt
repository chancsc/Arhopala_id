Arhopala vihara is a moderately large, tailed species belonging to the monotypic vihara group. Its identification on the underside is based on the following keys and features:

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is distinctive for being rounded and more or less separated from the adjacent spots.

Forewing Postdiscal Band: 
The band is strongly dislocated at vein 4, meaning the segment containing spots 2 and 3 is significantly offset from the segment containing spots 4, 5, and 6.

However, the postdiscal spots in spaces 4, 5, and 6 are in continuation (arranged in a line or even curve) with each other.

Missing Markings: It lacks a costal spot in space 10 on the forewing underside.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Tornal Area: The hindwing underside features tornal green scales.

Ground Color: The ground color is described as hair brown.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark violet blue with a narrow regular border.

Female: Brighter blue than the male.
