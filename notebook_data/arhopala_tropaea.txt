Arhopala tropaea is a very small and rare member of the amphimuta group and the muta subgroup. It is considered endemic to the Malay Peninsula.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside has a hair brown ground colour.

Marking Style: As a member of the muta subgroup, the markings are characteristically quadrate and banded.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 are arranged in an echelon, with the spot in space 2 stepped out significantly towards the wing edge (termen).

Hindwing Markings:
The postdiscal band is widely dislocated at vein 2.

The inner edge of the spot in space 2 is positioned in line with, or only slightly outside, the outer edge of the spot in space 1b.

Structural Features: It is a tailless species.

Size: A very small species with a forewing length of only 13.0–14.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned specifically nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar. This distinguishes it from species where the spot overlaps the end-cell bar or is placed centrally.

Upperside Characteristics

Female: Typically follows the subgroup pattern with broader black borders, though specific female descriptions for this rare species are limited.
