Arhopala myrzalina is a very rare member of the camdeo group and the myrzala subgroup. It is a small, tailless species characterized by the following underside features:

Underside Identification Keys

Coloration: The ground color is crimson brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are outlined by distinct white striae.

Hindwing Specifics: 
It possesses a spot at the extreme base of space 6.

The central and outer spots in space 7 are conjoined at their upper ends.

The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Forewing Specifics: 
There is a dark area under the cell, outwardly defined by a pale line stretching across space 1b.

It lacks a spot at the base of space 3.

Size: It is a small species with a forewing length of approximately 16.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the myrzala subgroup, the centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The spot in space 6 is positioned with its inner edge in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining blue with broad black borders (1.5 mm at the tornus expanding to 4.0 mm at the costa), giving it an appearance very similar to a female.

Female: Similar to the male.
