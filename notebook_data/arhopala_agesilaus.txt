Arhopala agesilaus gesa is a member of the agesilaus group, which consists of small, tailless species that can sometimes be confused with the amphimuta group.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings: Unlike its close relative Arhopala avatha, it possesses a basal spot in the cell.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Structural Features: It is a tailless species.

Size: A small species with a forewing length of approximately 20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the agesilaus group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned roughly midway between the postdiscal spot in space 5 and the end-cell bar. 

Diagnostic Value: This midway placement is a key character for the group, distinguishing it from species where the spot overlaps or is much closer to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Rich violet blue with broad black borders measuring 2.0 mm at the forewing tornus and increasing to 4.0 mm at the apex.

Female: Brighter blue than the male.
