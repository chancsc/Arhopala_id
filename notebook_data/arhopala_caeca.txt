Arhopala caeca is the rarest and most beautiful member of the aurea group. It is distinguished by its incomplete underside markings and the distinctive upperside coloration of the male.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are notably incomplete and the postdiscal band is narrow.

Forewing Markings: 
It lacks both the basal and central spots in the cell.

The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 are wider than those positioned above vein 4, with the spot in space 3 being especially wide.

The dark patch located in space 1b is not solid.

Hindwing Markings: 
A variable number of basal and discal spots are missing.

The postdiscal band is narrow and only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Structural Features: 
The hindwing cell is exceptionally long, measuring more than half the length of the wing.

The forewing termen is typically straight or slightly concave below vein 5.

It features a short, stout tail that is just under 2.0 mm in length.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are overlapping and arranged in an echelon (forming a more or less straight line).

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Brilliant green, which is overlaid with violet in the tornal areas of both the forewing and hindwing. The black border is reduced to a thread.

Female: Typically blue with broad black borders, resembling other members of its group.
