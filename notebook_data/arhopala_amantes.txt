Arhopala amantes is a large, tailed member of the centaurus group, primarily found in South Myanmar. It is distinguished from its close relatives by its specific wing shape and the extension of dark patches on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Features: Like other members of the centaurus group, the hindwing cell is short (much less than half the wing length), and the cell spots are characteristically outlined with greenish silver.

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted outwards (distad), placing it out of line with the spots above and below it.

The species lacks a costal spot in space 10.

A prominent dark patch under the cell in the upper part of space 1a extends all the way to the postdiscal band.

Structural Features: The hindwing has a well-developed tornal lobe and a filamentous tail at vein 2.

Size: A large species with a forewing length of approximately 27.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned specifically nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining blue with exceptionally broad black borders, measuring 3.0 mm at the forewing tornus and increasing to 5.0 mm at the apex.

Female: A lighter shade of blue.
