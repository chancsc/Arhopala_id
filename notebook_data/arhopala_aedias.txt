Arhopala aedias (the Large Metallic Oakblue) is a namesake member of the aedias group. It is one of the largest species in the genus and is characterized by its uniform underside pattern and long tail.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings and spots are narrowly outlined with pale buff lines.

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: 
The postdiscal spots in spaces 5, 6, and 7 are arranged in an echelon (in a line or series of overlapping steps).

The spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Forewing Markings: 
It lacks the well-defined dark area under the cell that is characteristic of the camdeo group.

The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted distad (outwards), moving it out of line with the spots in spaces 5 and 6.

The forewing postdiscal band is complete, continuing into space 9 and not ending early at vein 7.

There is no central spot in forewing underside space 11 — this species belongs to a group lacking subcostal spots in spaces 10 and 11 (a spot is rarely present in space 10).

The underside ground colour is normal (a uniform hair brown), not extensively whitened.

Hindwing postdiscal spot 6 is not smaller than spot 7; in this group spot 6 is typically as large as or larger than spot 7 (the 'spot 6 smaller than spot 7' character is instead diagnostic for A. camdana and A. semperi).

Tail: It possesses a long filamentous tail at vein 2, measuring approximately 6.0 mm.

Size and Variation: It occurs in two size forms: a large form (forewing length 25.0–29.0 mm) and a small form (20.0–22.0 mm). Smaller individuals often display more rounded and darker spots on the underside.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining bluish purple with a very narrow black border (a thread).

Female: Shining bluish purple with broad black borders.

Subspecies Note: In the Langkawi subspecies meritatas, the upperside is paler and brighter blue in both sexes compared to the common mainland subspecies agnis.
