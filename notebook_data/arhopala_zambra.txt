Arhopala zambra zambra is a member of the cleander group. It is distinguished from other large species in its group by specific alignments of the postdiscal spots and the width of markings on the forewing.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings: 
The costal spot in space 10 is usually as wide as the end-cell bar and is positioned immediately above it.

The postdiscal spot in space 9 is as wide as, or wider than, the spot in space 6 and is placed in continuation of the spots in spaces 4, 5, and 6. This differs from related species where the spot in space 9 is typically narrower and shifted inward.

Hindwing Markings: 
The postdiscal band is dislocated at vein 2.

It features a large, conspicuously projecting tornal lobe.

General Appearance: The underside is typically hair brown with the usual arrangement of darker markings outlined by pale striae.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the cleander group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement relative to Spot 7: Postdiscal spots 6 and 7 are oblique and arranged in an echelon (placed one below the other). The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The outer edge of the spot is straight or convex.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deep blue with the black border reduced to a thread. A rarer form exists that is more violet-blue.

Female: Typical of the genus with broad black borders and a purple-blue ground colour.
