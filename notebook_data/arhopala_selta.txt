Arhopala selta (the Selta Oakblue) is a member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is primarily distinguished from its close relatives by its specific wing shape and the overlap of its underside markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are not conspicuously darker than the ground colour and are outlined by pale buff striae.

Forewing Shape: The forewing has a rounded termen, distinguishing it from related species with straighter wing edges.

Hindwing Blue: On the upperside, the blue colour does not reach the termen in space 6, which is a useful character to verify when the underside is visible.

Subspecies: Arhopala selta selta is found in Kedah, while Arhopala selta hislopi occurs in Borneo.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the alea subgroup, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Overlap: A key diagnostic feature for Arhopala selta is that the postdiscal spot in space 6 widely overlaps the end-cell bar. This helps separate it from species like Arhopala phaenops, where the spot usually does not overlap the bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Violet blue with a black border that measures 0.75 mm at the tornus and expands to nearly 1.5 mm at the apex. The hindwing border is approximately 1.25 mm.

Female: Purplish blue with the typical broad black borders.
