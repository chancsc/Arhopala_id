Arhopala metamuta metamuta is a member of the amphimuta group and the perimuta subgroup. It is particularly notable for the striking contrast between its forewing and hindwing colours on the upperside.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Hindwing Scales: It possesses distinct tornal green scales on the hindwing.

Forewing Markings: Unlike the muta subgroup, the postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 on the forewing are in line (though they may be slightly oblique), rather than being stepped or in echelon.

Subgroup Characteristics: As part of the perimuta subgroup, the male genitalia feature narrow, bilobate valvae and short, broad dorsal hooks.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned specifically nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: The forewing is a dark, shining violet blue with a 1.5 mm black border. The hindwing is a brilliant greenish blue, which provides a strong and diagnostic contrast against the darker forewing.

Female: Shining blue with a violet tint; the black border on the hindwing is very regular.
