Arhopala ariana wilcocksi is a member of the alitaeus group. It is generally rare on the peninsular mainland but can be found in the Langkawi Islands.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is rather dark brown and usually features a faint purplish wash.

Forewing Markings: 
It possesses a costal spot in mid-space 11.

The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted distad (outwards), placing it out of line with the spots in spaces 5 and 6.

Structural Features: The forewing termen is slightly convex, and the apex is not produced. It is a tailed species.

Hindwing Band: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the alitaeus group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is characteristically straight or convex, a key feature distinguishing this group from others where the spot may be concave.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Blue with a black border measuring 1.0–1.5 mm at the tornus and increasing slightly at the apex.

Female: Blue with broader black borders.
