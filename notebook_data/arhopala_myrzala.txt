Arhopala myrzala is a member of the camdeo group and specifically the myrzala subgroup. It is characterized by its distinctive crimson-brown ground color and specific spot alignments on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is a rich crimson-brown.

Marking Definition: All markings are clearly outlined by white striae.

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: 
The center of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is more or less in line with the centers of the spots in spaces 7 and 5 (arranged in an echelon).

The spot in space 6 is positioned specifically nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Additional Hindwing Features:
A spot is always present at the base of space 6.

The central and outer postdiscal spots in space 7 are conjoined at their upper ends.

Forewing Markings:
There is a dark area under the cell, defined by a pale line stretching across space 1b.

A spot at the base of space 3 is present less frequently than in other subgroups of the camdeo group.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2, which distinguishes it from the similar but tailless species Arhopala myrzalina.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: The upperside is shining purplish blue. In subspecies lammas, the black border is narrow, while in the Langkawi subspecies conjuncta, the color is bluer and the border is slightly wider.

Female: Typically more purple with broader black borders.
