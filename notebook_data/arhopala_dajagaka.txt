============================================================

Arhopala dajagaka is a member of the amphimuta group and the amphimuta subgroup. It is primarily a Bornean species and is considered very difficult to distinguish from its close relative Arhopala amphimuta without examining the male genitalia.

Underside Identification Keys

Postdiscal Band: On the hindwing, the postdiscal band is partially dislocated at vein 2.

Cilia and Wing Edge: 
The hindwing cilia feature a small white spot in space 1a.

CORRECTION (2026-06-17, iNat field-photo review): the white-spot-in-space-1a claim above does not hold up against field photos — only A. norda and A. moolaiana actually show this spot. Treat this line as unreliable; do not use it as a Feature Scoring feature for A. dajagaka.

The hindwing has a very weak tooth at vein 2.

Size: A relatively large species for its group, with a forewing length of 21.0–25.0 mm.

Marking Definition: The markings follow the typical subgroup pattern, featuring quadrate and banded spots on a hair brown ground.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar. 

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark bluish purple with a narrow black border measuring 0.5 mm.

Female: Purple with a black hindwing border that is at least 2.0 mm wide.
