============================================================

Arhopala sceva indra is a rare member of the amphimuta group and the amphimuta subgroup. It is a small to medium-sized, tailless species that has only recently been recorded in the Malay Peninsula.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: Typically hair brown with a slightly glazed appearance.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is more or less dislocated at vein 2.

Cilia Characteristics: The hindwing cilia feature a small white spot in space 1a and a very weak tooth at vein 2.

CORRECTION (2026-06-17, iNat field-photo review): the white-spot-in-space-1a claim above does not hold up against field photos — only A. norda and A. moolaiana actually show this spot. Treat this line as unreliable; do not use it as a Feature Scoring feature for A. sceva.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal spot in space 4 is in continuation of the spots in spaces 5 and 6, rather than being shifted outwards.

Size: The forewing length is approximately 18.0–20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark violet blue with a black forewing border that measures 0.75 mm at the tornus and expands to 1.25 mm at the apex.

Female: Purple with large black borders (though the female form has not yet been formally recorded within the Malay Peninsula).
