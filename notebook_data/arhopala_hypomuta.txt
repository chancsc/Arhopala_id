Arhopala hypomuta hypomuta is the sole member of the hypomuta group. It is a tailless species characterized by several diagnostic underside features.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Cilia: There is a small white spot in space 1a.

Hindwing Tooth: The hindwing has a very weak tooth at vein 2, which can be inconspicuous in worn specimens.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is partially dislocated at vein 2.

Male Genitalia: The male possesses a unilobate valva, which helps distinguish it from some members of the amphimuta group that have bilobate valvae.

Size: It is a medium-sized species with a forewing length of approximately 19.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning their centres are more or less in a straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deep, shining violet blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Shining purple blue with the typical broad black borders common to the genus.
