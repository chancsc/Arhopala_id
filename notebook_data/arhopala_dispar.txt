Arhopala dispar pendleburyi is a very rare species belonging to the camdeo group and camdeo subgroup. It is uniquely characterized by its extensively whitened underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside ground colour is mostly white, which is a distinctive diagnostic feature.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal band is complete but completely dislocated at vein 4.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species with a notably long tail, measuring approximately 4.0–6.0 mm.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of approximately 25.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the camdeo group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Pale greyish blue and noticeably whitish about the end of the cell; the black border is reduced to a thread.

Female: Pale blue and even more extensively whitened than the male.
