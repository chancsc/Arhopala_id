Arhopala azinis azinis is a very rare montane species belonging to the agrata group and the ace subgroup. It is characterized by specific band-like markings and the shape of its postdiscal spots.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Central Cell Spot: The central cell spot is band-like and stretches entirely across the cell.

Forewing Postdiscal Band: The band is sinuous or only slightly dislocated at vein 4.

General Markings: The species shares the distinctive markings of the ace subgroup, featuring wide and clearly defined spots.

Size: It is smaller than its relative Arhopala ace, with a forewing length of approximately 19.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Placement relative to Spot 5: The postdiscal spot in space 6 just overlaps the postdiscal spot in space 5.

Placement relative to End-Cell Bar: The spot in space 6 does not reach the end-cell bar.

Shape: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is straight or convex. This is a primary diagnostic feature used to separate it from Arhopala ace, which has an outwardly concave spot 6.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark blue with broad black borders measuring 2.0 mm at the forewing mid-termen and 3.0 mm on the hindwing.

Female: Purple.
