Arhopala anella is a member of the abseus group. It is a relatively large species distinguished by its specific wing venation and the arrangement of spots on the hindwing.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristics:
Possesses a short, white-tipped tail at vein 3 of the hindwing.

Features a spot at the base of space 1a on the hindwing underside.

Vein 9 of the forewing is very short, arising well beyond the end of vein 10.

Ground Colour: The underside is uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are not prominently darkened, which distinguishes it from the closely related Arhopala abseus.

Forewing Markings: It lacks a costal spot in space 12 on the forewing underside.

Size: It is a large species with a forewing length of approximately 20.0–26.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the abseus group (which shares the pattern of the ganesa group), the centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned with its inner edge in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: The blue areas are restricted, confined to the basal halves of spaces 1b, 2, and 3 and the lower half of the cell on the forewing, and almost entirely to the cell on the hindwing.

Female: Violet blue with relatively narrow black borders.
