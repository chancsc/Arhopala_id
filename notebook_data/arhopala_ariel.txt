Arhopala ariel is a very rare and small member of the ganesa group. It is primarily recorded from lowland forest areas.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
Possesses a spot at the base of space 11, situated between the basal and central cell spots.

Features a spot at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
Tornal green scales are present.

The gap between the outer and central spots in space 7 is rarely completely whitened (distinguishing it from A. ammonides).

The outer and central spots in space 7 are joined at their upper ends.

Ground Colour: Typically a shade of purple brown.

Size: A very small species with a forewing length of approximately 13.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the ganesa group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Specific Extent: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is "normal," meaning it does not reach the base of the space. This is a key diagnostic feature used to separate it from Arhopala ammon, where spot 6 is continued to the base of the space.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Bluish purple and hardly shining. It features broad black borders: 1.5 mm at the forewing tornus expanding to 4.0 mm at the apex, and 2.0 mm on the hindwing.

Female: Generally bluer than the male with even broader black borders.
