Arhopala opalina azata is a member of the camdeo group and the camdeo subgroup. It is a relatively large species with several distinguishing features on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
Features a distinctive dark area under the cell, outwardly defined by a pale line that normally stretches across space 1b.

As a member of the camdeo subgroup, it frequently possesses a small spot at the extreme base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
The central spot in space 1b is not unusually elongate.

The lower end of the end-cell bar is not typically enlarged outwards.

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Size: Forewing length measures approximately 22.0–26.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line).

Size and Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is as large as, or larger than, the spot in space 7. It is positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Pale violet blue with a noticeable greyish tinge; the black border is reduced to a thread.

Female: A darker shade of blue than the male.
