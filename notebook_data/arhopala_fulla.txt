Arhopala fulla (represented by subspecies Arhopala fulla intaca in Malaysia) belongs to the small, mainly Papuan fulla group. It is distinguished by its small size and the following features on the wing underside:

Underside Identification Keys

Reduced Markings: This species is instantly recognizable because the usual series of distinct postdiscal spots is replaced by a single obscure postdiscal fascia on both wings.

Missing Features: It typically lacks a bar at the end of the cell on the forewing, and sometimes this bar is also missing on the hindwing.

Basal Spots: Most markings are absent except for a few obscure, small spots located in the basal area of the hindwing.

Tail Status: It is a tailless species.

Ground Color: The underside is brown, often with a slight purple wash.

Hindwing Spot 6 Position

In Arhopala fulla, specific positions for individual postdiscal spots, such as spot 6, are not clearly defined as they are in other species. This is because the typical spots are suppressed and merged into the continuous, obscure postdiscal fascia mentioned above. According to the genus identification keys, it is categorized among the species where markings are largely confined to these faint fasciae.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining violet blue with a thread-like black border.

Female: Deeper violet with broader black borders.
