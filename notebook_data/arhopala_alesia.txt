Arhopala alesia sacharja is a very rare species, recently recorded as a late entry in the Malay Peninsula (specifically from Langkawi). It belongs to the agelastus group and the alesia subgroup.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristics: As a member of the agelastus group, the postdiscal band on the forewing is continuous, and the postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Subgroup Characteristics: Diagnostic features for the alesia subgroup include:
Submarginal Markings: The inner submarginal spots in spaces 2 and 3 on the forewing and spaces 4 and 5 on the hindwing are notably enlarged and darkened.

Structural Features: tailed

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned below the spot in space 7.

Shape/Position: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is characteristically outwardly concave or sinuous. This serves as a primary diagnostic feature to distinguish members of the alesia subgroup from the agelastus subgroup (where spot 6 is outwardly convex).

CORRECTION (2026-06-17, field-photo review): the above spot-6 shape/position and submarginal-spot claims do not hold up against field photos of A. alesia sacharja. In actual specimens: the HW spaces 4–5 submarginal spots are NOT enlarged (uniform in size, like A. barami penanga); spot 6 touches/overlaps the end-cell bar (displaced, not "in the normal postdiscal row"); and the outer edge of spot 6 is curved outward (convex), not concave/sinuous. This makes A. alesia sacharja's underside markings effectively identical to A. barami penanga's, not to A. alaconia media's — the literature's "alesia subgroup = concave spot 6 + enlarged spots" framing should be treated as unreliable for A. alesia sacharja specifically. Use forewing length and male upperside border width (this species: smaller, border ~3.0 mm at tornus, not reaching A. barami penanga's exceptional 8.0 mm at costa) to separate the two instead.

Field photo observation (confirmed 2026-06-17): HW postdiscal band only slightly dislocated at vein 2 — the two portions are shifted but still touching, not a clean, fully separated gap (consistent with the "only partially dislocated at vein 2" group characteristic above, ruling out a fully dislocated reading). Combined with the spot-6 and submarginal-spot corrections above, the confirmed underside picture for A. alesia sacharja is: HW postdiscal band slightly dislocated at vein 2 but still touching; spot 6 outwardly convex, touching/overlapping the end-cell bar; HW spaces 4–5 submarginal spots uniform, not enlarged.

Further field photo observation (iNat, confirmed 2026-06-17): underside ground colour is light brown. HW postdiscal spots in spaces 4 and 5, and the end-cell bar, are very pale — close to the ground colour, with little contrast — each ringed by a black border and then a white outer ring. The FW postdiscal band, by contrast, is almost entirely dark/black, strongly contrasting against the light brown ground colour.

CORRECTION (2026-06-18, field-photo review): the ground colour is more precisely described as pale grey-brown — distinctly paler than the typical hair-brown Arhopala underside — not just "light brown". This is a unique, decisive early-exit character: A. barami penanga (the only species this could otherwise be confused with) has a typical hair-brown underside with solid spots darker than the ground colour, not the pale grey-brown ground with low-contrast, black-then-white-ringed spots described above. Added as a new decisive question (after the macular-markings check) in data/tree.json, letting A. alesia sacharja exit the key immediately rather than waiting for the final forewing-size tie-break against A. barami penanga.

Upperside Characteristics

Male and Female: The sexes are generally alike, featuring violet blue wings with a broad black forewing border that measures approximately 3.0 mm at the tornus and expands towards the mid-costa.
