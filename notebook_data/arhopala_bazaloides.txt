Arhopala bazaloides bazaloides is an Indo-Burmese species recorded in the Langkawi Islands. It belongs to the camdeo group and sitting within its own bazaloides subgroup, it is distinguished by a specific spot arrangement that is unique within the genus.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is purple brown.

Marking Definition: Markings are clearly outlined by whitish striae.

Unique Feature: It possesses a prominent spot in space 8 adjoining the central spot in space 7. This characteristic is unique among Arhopala species.

Hindwing Spot Arrangement:
It lacks a spot at the base of space 6.

The central and outer spots in space 7 are separate. This helps distinguish it from the myrzala subgroup, where these spots are conjoined at their upper ends.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of approximately 21.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the camdeo group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark violet blue with a regular black border measuring 2.0 mm in width.

Female: Typically follows the genus pattern with more purplish blue wings and broader black borders.
