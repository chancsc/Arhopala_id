Arhopala arianaga is a tailed member of the alitaeus group. It is a montane species, appearing only above 1,000 meters in the main range.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour and Glaze: The underside ground colour is hair brown, overlaid with a very faint pinkish glaze.

Marking Definition: The markings are a little darker than the ground colour and are better defined and more contrasted than those of its close relative, Arhopala elopura.

Forewing Markings: 
Possesses a costal spot mid-space 11.

The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted distad (outwards), placing it out of line with the spots in spaces 5 and 6.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species with a forewing length of approximately 21.0–22.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the alitaeus group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is characteristically straight or convex.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Slightly darker blue than the females of other Malayan species within the same group.
