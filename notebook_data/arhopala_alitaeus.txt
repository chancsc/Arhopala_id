Arhopala alitaeus (specifically subspecies mirabella and pardenas) is a member of the alitaeus group. It is characterized by macular markings and a distinct glaze on the wing underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
It possesses a central spot in space 11.

It features a spot at the extreme base of space 10, situated above the central cell spot.

The postdiscal spot in space 1b is typically dislocated outwards from the spot in space 2, whereas in related species these spots are usually continuous and oblique.

Marking Style: The markings are described as more annular (ring-like) or macular compared to preceding species in its group.

Glaze and Coloration: The ground color is a pale slatey purple. The strength of the glaze depends on the subspecies:
Subspecies mirabella (Malay Peninsula and Langkawi): Features a strong purplish glaze.

Subspecies pardenas (Singapore): Features a weak glaze.

Size: It is a relatively small species with a forewing length of approximately 16.0–17.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the alitaeus group, the centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Shape and Overlap: The spot in space 6 is rounded and typically overlaps the end-cell bar.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Blue with regular black borders that are at least 1.0 mm wide.
