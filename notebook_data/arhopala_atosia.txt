Arhopala atosia belongs to the epimuta group. Its identification on the underside is defined by the following keys and features:

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned approximately midway between the postdiscal spot in space 5 and the end-cell bar.

Hindwing Spot Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 5, 6, and 7 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line).

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are typically less defined and paler compared to the closely related Arhopala lurida, which has darker and better-defined markings.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2 of the hindwing (distinguishing it from the tailless Arhopala epimuta).

Size: It is a medium-sized species with a forewing length of approximately 22.0 mm.
