Arhopala araxes is a large member of the centaurus group. It is primarily found from Sumatra and Java to the Lesser Sunda Islands and the Sulawesi region. For a long time, it was identified in error by many authors as Arhopala pseudocentaurus.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristics: Like other members of the centaurus group, the hindwing cell is short, measuring much less than half the length of the wing.

Cell Markings: A primary diagnostic feature is that the forewing cell spots are distinctly outlined with greenish silver.

Forewing Markings:
The postdiscal band is continuous.

A costal spot is present in space 10.

Structural Features: The hindwing possesses a distinct tornal lobe. This helps distinguish it from the closely related Arhopala centaurus, in which the tornal lobe is vestigial and the tornus is rounded.

Size: A robust species with a forewing length of approximately 27.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining blue, overlaid with a violet tinge towards the wing apices. The black forewing border is a thread at the tornus and increases to 1.0 mm at the apex.

Female: Blue.
