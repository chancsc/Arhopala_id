Arhopala camdana camdana is a very rare member of the camdeo group and camdeo subgroup. It is characterized by its long tails and specific structural alignments on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform grey-brown to hair brown.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species with a notably long tail, measuring approximately 4.0–6.0 mm.

Size: A smaller species relative to A. semperi, with a forewing length of approximately 21.0 mm. Forewing length alone separates A. camdana camdana (~21.0 mm) from A. semperi russelli (~28.0 mm), even when the upperside cannot be examined.

Position and Size of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the camdeo group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Relative Size: A key diagnostic feature for Arhopala camdana is that the postdiscal spot in space 6 is smaller than the postdiscal spot in space 7.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Rather pale, clear violet blue. The black forewing border is narrow (0.5 mm) and scarcely increases towards the apex.

Female: Pale purple blue with a narrow black border on the hindwing.
