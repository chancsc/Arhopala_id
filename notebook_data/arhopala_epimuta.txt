Arhopala epimuta (Common Disc Oakblue) is a member of the epimuta group. It is distinguished by the following features on the wing underside:

Underside Identification Keys

Tail Status: It is a tailless species, which distinguishes it from its close relative Arhopala atosia.

Hindwing Shape: The hindwing tornus is rounded.

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned approximately midway between the postdiscal spot in space 5 and the end-cell bar. 

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is widely dislocated at vein 2.

Forewing Markings: 
The markings are variable, and the postdiscal spots may be missing below vein 3.

The postdiscal spot in space 3, when fully developed, is elongated towards the base.

The spot in space 1b, when present, is placed midway between the end-cell bar and the postdiscal spot in space 2.

Marking Definition: In this group, the underside markings are typically darker and better defined compared to related species like A. atosia.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Features a large circular discal patch on the forewing that contrasts feebly with the rest of the blue wing. The black border is reduced to a thread.

Female: Possesses broad black borders.
