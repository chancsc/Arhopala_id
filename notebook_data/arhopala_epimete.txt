Arhopala epimete duessa is a member of the agelastus group and the sole representative of the epimete subgroup. It is a tailless species distributed from south Myanmar to Sundaland.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Postdiscal Band: A primary diagnostic feature is that the postdiscal band increases significantly in width from the costa down to vein 2. Consequently, the lower portion of the band (in spaces 2 and 3) is much wider than the upper part.

Costal Markings: A costal spot is present in space 10 on the forewing.

Hindwing Band: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Tornal Markings: It lacks tornal green scales on the hindwing.

Ground Colour: The underside is brown, typically overlaid with a purple wash.

Structural Features: It is a tailless species.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: Consistent with its group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Sexes: The male and female are alike in appearance.

Colour: Both are blue with broad black borders on the forewing.

Hindwing Blue: The blue coloration on the hindwing is restricted and confined to the cell.
