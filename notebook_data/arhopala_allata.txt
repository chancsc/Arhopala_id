Arhopala allata pandora is a rather rare species belonging to the predominantly Indo-Burmese oenea group. It is characterized by its acute forewing shape and rectangular hindwing markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Structure: The forewing apex is acute and the termen is straight between veins 2 and 4.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal band is sinuous or only slightly dislocated at vein 4.

Hindwing Markings: The postdiscal spots in spaces 7 and 6 are distinctively narrow and roughly rectangular.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species.

Size: The forewing length is approximately 22.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned specifically nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark purple with a black forewing border that measures 0.5 mm at the tornus and expands to 1.5 mm at the apex.

Female: A dark, shining blue.
