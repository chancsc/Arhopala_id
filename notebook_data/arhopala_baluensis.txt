Arhopala baluensis is a Bornean endemic species belonging to the amphimuta group and amphimuta subgroup. It is remarkably similar to Arhopala amphimuta and can be very difficult to distinguish from small individuals of that species without examining the male genitalia.

Underside Identification Keys

Postdiscal Band: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is partially dislocated at vein 2.

Cilia Characteristics: 
The hindwing cilia typically lack a white spot in space 1a (if present, it is very rare and obscure).

The hindwing is very weakly toothed at vein 2.

Genitalia: The male valva is unilobate.

Size: A small to medium-sized species with a forewing length of 18.0–20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark purple-blue with a narrow black border measuring approximately 0.5 mm.

Female: Purple-blue with borders that are narrower than is typical for the genus.
