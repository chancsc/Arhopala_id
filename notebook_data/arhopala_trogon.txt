Arhopala trogon is a robust member of the aurea group. It is characterized by its purple-washed underside and a distinctive long hindwing cell.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color and Markings: The underside is brown with a prominent purple wash. The markings are complete and well-defined.

Hindwing Cell: The hindwing cell is exceptionally long, measuring considerably more than half the length of the wing in the male.

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 are wider than those above vein 4.

The postdiscal spot in space 4 is shifted distad (outwards), moving it out of line with the spots in spaces 5 and 6.

The dark patch typically found under the cell in space 1b is not solid.

Tail: It possesses a short and stout tail at vein 2, measuring just under 2.0 mm.

Similar Species: The underside of A. trogon exactly resembles that of Arhopala stinga, but the two are distinguished by their upperside coloration.

Hindwing Spot 6 Position

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 has its inner edge positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7. 

Overlapping: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Brilliant, brassy green with a strong blue suffusion at the wing bases. The black border is reduced to a thread. On the hindwing, the green area usually reaches the termen in spaces 4 and 5.

Female: Shining blue with broad black borders, resembling the female of Arhopala aurea.
