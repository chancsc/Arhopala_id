Arhopala alica is a member of the amphimuta group. It is a rare species, primarily known from Borneo (recorded in Keningau, Sabah), and is characterized by its rounded hindwing and specific underside alignments.

Underside Identification Keys

Structural Features: It is a tailless species with an evenly rounded hindwing. The hindwing cilia lack any trace of a tooth at vein 2.

Underside Markings: Markings almost entirely absent. Only a faint trace of the HW postdiscal marking is visible; no postdiscal streak or band present. This degree of suppression is more extreme than A. fulla intaca (which retains a faint obscure postdiscal streak) and is diagnostic for the species in field photos.

Postdiscal Band: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is typically partially dislocated at vein 2, though this character may not be absolutely constant.

Size: The forewing length is approximately 17.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned specifically nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Field Observation Note (as observed in field photos): Spot 6 position cannot be reliably determined from available field photos. Character marked as Cannot determine.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark, dull purple (described in some records as dark bluish purple) with the black border reduced to a thread or a very fine line.

Female: Purple with a broad black border.
