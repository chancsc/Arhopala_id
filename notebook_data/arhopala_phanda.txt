Arhopala phanda phanda is a member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is known for its close similarity to other species in its subgroup, requiring specific underside alignments for identification.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is hair brown, occasionally featuring a faint rosy wash.

Marking Definition: The markings are not conspicuously darker than the ground colour and are outlined by pale buff striae.

Postdiscal Band Proximity: The postdiscal band is placed close to the inner submarginal lunules on both wings.

Forewing Markings: 
The costal spot in space 10 is usually absent.

The postdiscal band is usually continuous (not, or only slightly, dislocated at vein 4).

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the alea subgroup, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Shape: The spot in space 6 is quadrate.

Placement relative to Spot 5: The outer edge of spot 6 usually just overlaps, or is in line with, the inner edge of the spot in space 5. This helps distinguish it from other subgroup members where the outer edge of spot 6 is typically well inside the inner edge of spot 5.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Darker and more violet in shade than Arhopala evansi. 

Female: The black forewing border is usually about 2.0 mm wide from vein 4 to the tornus.
