Arhopala paraganesa mendava is a small, rare member of the ganesa group. In the Malay Peninsula, this species is entirely montane and is distinguished by several unique underside markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
It possesses a small spot at the base of space 11, positioned between the basal and central cell spots.

A spot is present at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
Features a distinctive, roughly quadrate whitish area extending from the costa to the mid-cell.

It lacks tornal green scales, which are present in many other members of the genus.

Structural Features: It possesses a short tail at vein 2.

Size: Small species, with a forewing length of approximately 15.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the ganesa group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Bright, pale blue with broad black borders measuring 2.0 mm at the forewing tornus and increasing to 4.0 mm at the apex. The hindwing is mostly fuscous (dark brown).

Female: Similar to the male but can be distinguished by a whitish patch located around the cell-end on the forewing.
