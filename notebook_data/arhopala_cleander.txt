Arhopala cleander aphadantas is a large species belonging to the cleander group and the cleander subgroup. It is characterized by its significant size and specific postdiscal alignments on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal band is not dislocated at vein 4, although it may be sinuous. This feature distinguishes it from Arhopala silhetensis, where the band is partially dislocated at that vein.

Hindwing Markings: 
The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 2.

It features a large and conspicuously projecting tornal lobe.

Size: A relatively large species with a forewing length of approximately 23.0–24.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Overlap: The postdiscal spot in space 6 does not overlap the end-cell bar. 

Diagnostic Gap: The interval (gap) between spot 6 and the end-cell bar is wider than the width of the spot itself. Similarly, the gap between the postdiscal spot in space 5 and the end-cell bar is wider than either of those spots.

Alignment: The postdiscal spots in spaces 6 and 7 are notably oblique. The inner edge of the spot in space 7 is positioned well inside the outer edge of the spot in space 6.

Shape: Spot 6 is wider at the top than at the bottom, and its outer edge is sinuous. 

Placement relative to Spot 5: The outer edge of spot 6 is placed inside the inner edge of the spot in space 5.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Violet blue with the black forewing border measuring 1.0–2.0 mm at the tornus and increasing to 2.0–3.0 mm at the apex.

Female: Typical of the genus with broad black borders.
