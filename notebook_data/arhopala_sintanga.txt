Arhopala sintanga tani is a member of the alitaeus group. It is represented in the Malay Peninsula by the subspecies tani, which was described based on its distinctive deeper violet upperside compared to its close relatives.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour and Glaze: The ground colour is a distinctive pale slatey purple.

Marking Definition: The markings are contrasting reddish brown.

Forewing Markings: A primary diagnostic feature is the presence of a spot at the extreme base of space 10 (located above the central cell spot). This feature distinguishes it from the nominate subspecies and the similar A. havilandi kota.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species, with the tail at vein 2 measuring approximately 3.5–4.0 mm.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of about 20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: Following the alitaeus group pattern, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 characteristically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deeper dark violet than the sympatric A. havilandi kota, with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: The female of the Malayan subspecies tani has not yet been recorded, but in the nominate subspecies from Borneo, the female has very wide forewing borders and an entirely brown hindwing.
