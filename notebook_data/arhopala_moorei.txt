============================================================

Arhopala moorei busa belongs to the amphimuta group and the muta subgroup. It is a tailless species that is very closely related to Arhopala muta.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spot 6 Position:
The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line).

Forewing Postdiscal Band: 
The postdiscal spot in space 2 is distinctly stepped out towards the termen, placing it in echelon with the spot in space 3.

The postdiscal spots are relatively quadrate and banded in appearance.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band is dislocated such that the inner edge of the spot in space 2 is in line with, or only a little outside, the outer edge of the spot in space 1b.

Tail Status: It is a tailless species.

Hindwing Cilia: The white spot in space 1a of the hindwing cilia is either absent or, very rarely, present but extremely obscure.

Genitalia: The male possesses an unilobate valva.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Both wings are the same shade of blue in all lights. This is a key diagnostic feature to separate it from Arhopala muta, which has a hindwing that is typically a slightly paler and brighter blue than the forewing.

Female: Bluish purple with the usual broad black borders.
