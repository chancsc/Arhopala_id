Arhopala johoreana johoreana is a rare member of the camdeo group. It is distinguished from its close relative Arhopala belphoebe by its darker upperside and the presence of a hindwing tail.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Band: The postdiscal band is incomplete and characteristically ends at vein 7.

Forewing Markings: 
It features a dark area positioned under the cell that is outwardly defined by a pale line stretching right across space 1b.

A small spot is often present at the extreme base of space 3.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species, possessing a short tail at vein 2.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of approximately 18.0–20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the camdeo group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, forming a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Coloration: Dark violet blue, which is significantly darker than the pale violet blue of Arhopala belphoebe.

Borders: Both sexes possess broad black borders that expand on the forewing to approximately 5.0 mm at the costa.
