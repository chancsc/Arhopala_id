Arhopala ace ace is a very rare species belonging to the agrata group and the ace subgroup. It is primarily distinguished by its wide underside markings and unique cell-spot arrangement.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Width: All markings on the underside are notably wide and prominent.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal band on the forewing is dislocated at vein 4.

Hindwing Central Cell Spot: The central cell spot is band-like and stretches entirely across the cell.

Tornal Features: Brassy tornal scales are present at the hindwing tornus.

Size: Medium-sized for the genus, with a forewing length of approximately 22.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement relative to End-Cell Bar: Unlike many other members of the Arhopala section, the postdiscal spot in space 6 does not reach the end-cell bar.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 just overlaps the postdiscal spot in space 5.

Shape: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is outwardly concave.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of spot 6 is positioned in line with, or further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deep cobalt blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Purple.
