Arhopala semperi russelli is a large species belonging to the camdeo group and the camdeo subgroup. It is characterized by its duller upperside and specific structural alignments on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
Features a dark area under the cell, outwardly defined by a pale line that normally stretches right across space 1b.

The postdiscal spot in space 9 is shifted basad, out of line with the spots in spaces 4, 5, and 6.

The postdiscal band is only partially dislocated at vein 4.

Hindwing Markings:
The central spot in space 1b is characteristically elongate.

The lower half of the end-cell bar is enlarged outwards.

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform grey-brown to hair brown.

Size: A large species with a forewing length of approximately 28.0 mm.

Position and Size of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the camdeo group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Relative Size: In Arhopala semperi, the postdiscal spot in space 6 is smaller than the postdiscal spot in space 7. This helps distinguish it from related species where spot 6 may be larger or equal in size to spot 7.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deep, dull bluish purple with a black border that increases to 1.25 mm at the apex.

Female: Purple, with a distinct whitish patch at the end of the cell.
