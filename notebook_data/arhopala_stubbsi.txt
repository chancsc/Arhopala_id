Arhopala stubbsi is a rare member of the amphimuta group and the muta subgroup. It is primarily distinguished by its size and the specific width of its upperside borders compared to its closest relatives.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristics: As a member of the amphimuta group, the hindwing possesses a small tornal lobe, though the cilia lack a trace of a tooth at vein 2.

Postdiscal Band: On the hindwing, the postdiscal band is characteristically partially dislocated at vein 2.

General Pattern: The markings follow the typical subgroup arrangement of quadrate and banded spots on a hair brown ground colour.

Size: A small species with a forewing length of approximately 16.5 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, meaning they overlap and their centres form a more or less straight line.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned specifically nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Rather dull bluish purple. It is distinguished from the similar Arhopala zylda and Arhopala alica by its much wider black border, which measures 2.0 mm.

Female: Brighter blue than the male with the typical broad black borders of the genus.
