Arhopala agaba belongs to the cleander group and specifically the agaba subgroup. It is characterized by its distinctive subapical markings and specific spot alignments on the hindwing underside.

Underside Identification Keys and Features

Ground Colour and Texture: The underside is pale purple-brown with dark markings. A key feature is the subapical area of both wings, which is usually irrorated (sprinkled) with whitish scales, making it appear paler than the rest of the wing.

Hindwing Spot 6 Position: 
The postdiscal spot in space 6 has its inner edge positioned in line with, or even inside, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

It typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Hindwing Spot 5: The postdiscal spot in space 5 is almost always contiguous or amalgamated with the end-cell bar.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining violet blue with a black border approximately 1.0 mm wide.

Female: Brighter blue than the male with broader black borders.
