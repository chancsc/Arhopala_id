Arhopala aurelia is a member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is characterized by its distinctive glazed ground colour and the specific alignment of its underside spots.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour and Glaze: The underside has a distinctive pale slatey purple ground colour.

Marking Definition: It features conspicuous dark markings that are clearly outlined by purplish white striae (lines).

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal band is usually continuous.

A costal spot in space 10 is normally present on the forewing.

Structural Features: It is a tailed species with a forewing length of approximately 19.0–20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the alea subgroup, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Shape: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is quadrate.

Placement relative to Spot 5: The outer edge of spot 6 usually just overlaps, or is in line with, the inner edge of the postdiscal spot in space 5. This specific alignment is a primary diagnostic feature used to separate it from several other members of its subgroup.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark, shining violet blue. The black border measures 0.5 mm at the forewing tornus and expands to 1.0 mm at the apex.

Female: Purple blue with the usual broad black borders typical of the genus.
