Arhopala phaenops sandakani is a member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is a medium-sized species found at low to moderate elevations.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are not conspicuously darker than the ground colour and are outlined by pale buff striae.

Forewing Shape: The central part of the forewing termen is characteristically straight.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of 18.0–20.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the alea subgroup, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Overlap: Unlike some closely related species, the postdiscal spot in space 6 usually does not overlap the end-cell bar, or only just touches it.

Placement relative to Spot 5: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned well inside the inner edge of the spot in space 5.

Upperside Characteristics
