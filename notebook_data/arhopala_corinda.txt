Arhopala corinda acestes belongs to the monotypic corinda group. It is a moderately large species that can be identified by the following underside keys:

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is hair brown and sometimes features a purple wash.

Wing Shape: The male forewing has a rather pointed apex and a straight termen. Generally, the forewing termen is straight or slightly concave below vein 5.

Hindwing Cell: The hindwing cell is longer than half the length of the wing.

Forewing Markings:
The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 are wider than those located above vein 4, with the spot in space 3 being particularly wide.

There is a large, solid, unbroken purple-brown patch beneath the cell in the basal half of space 1b.

It lacks a costal spot in space 10.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at vein 2, measuring approximately 3.5 mm in length.

Size: A relatively large species with a forewing length of 21.0–24.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The spot is notably convex.

Differentiation from the aurea group (A. trogon, A. stinga, A. borneensis, A. aurea)

Both A. corinda and the aurea group have the HW cell longer than half the wing — this shared
character separates both groups from other Arhopala. They are told apart as follows:

- HW cell: in the aurea group (males) the cell is CONSIDERABLY longer than half the wing,
  combined with an acute or concave FW apex; in A. corinda the cell is longer than half but
  not to that degree (not "considerably" longer).
- Male upperside colour: A. corinda is deep purple/violet; aurea-group males are some shade of
  green (brassy green to green with blue suffusion).
- Tail: A. corinda has a long filamentous tail ~3.5 mm; aurea-group tails are shorter and
  stouter, just under 2.0 mm.
- FW underside space 1b patch: A. corinda has a large, solid, unbroken dark purple-brown patch
  in the basal half of space 1b; in the aurea group this dark patch is not solid.
- Size: A. corinda is larger (FW 21.0–24.0 mm) than the aurea group (FW ~16.0–21.0 mm).

Within the aurea group: A. trogon — brassy green male with strong blue basal suffusion,
underside purple-washed. A. stinga — green male with NO blue basal suffusion and a wider FW
border at the tornus, underside purple-washed. A. borneensis — brassier green male with strong
blue suffusion, green often reaching the HW termen in spaces 5 or 6, underside hair brown (not
purple-washed).

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Deep violet with a very narrow black border.

Female: Purple blue with the usual broad black borders.
