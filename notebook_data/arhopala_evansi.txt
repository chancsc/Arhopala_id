Arhopala evansi is a member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is distinguished from its close relatives by its specific glaze and the alignment of spots on both wings.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour and Glaze: The underside features a characteristic slatey pink glaze.

Marking Definition: The markings are outlined by striae that are notably whiter than those of other species in the alea subgroup (with the exception of Arhopala aurelia).

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 are arranged in an echelon, are upright, and are larger than the spots positioned above them.

Hindwing Band: The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Size: A medium-sized species with a forewing length of approximately 18.0–21.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As with other subgroup members, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Inner Edge Placement: The inner edge of spot 6 is positioned more or less in line with the outer edge of the end-cell bar.

Outer Edge Placement: The outer edge of spot 6 is positioned well inside the inner edge of the spot in space 5.

Shape: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is characteristically convex.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Blue with a violet tinge (though the violet is not very strong) and a narrow black border measuring 0.5 mm.

Female: Features a black forewing border that decreases in width towards the tornus.
