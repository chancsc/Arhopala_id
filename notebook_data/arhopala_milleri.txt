Arhopala milleri is a rare member of the cleander group and the alea subgroup. It is closely related to Arhopala sublustris but is larger in size and more matt in its upperside coloration.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: The markings are not conspicuously darker than the ground colour and are outlined by pale buff striae.

Forewing Markings: 
The costal spot in space 10 is usually absent.

The postdiscal band is usually continuous.

Size: Larger than its close relatives, with a forewing length averaging 19.5 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As part of the alea subgroup, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement relative to Spot 5: The outer edge of the postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned well inside the inner edge of the spot in space 5. This specific alignment helps distinguish it from Arhopala phanda, where the outer edge of spot 6 just overlaps or is in line with the inner edge of spot 5.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Darker and more matt indigo blue than Arhopala sublustris, with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Features a black forewing border that tapers towards the tornus.
